Building a Spelling Worksheet Generator as a Claude Skill

I've got a whole album side on all the ways AI in education is going to go wrong, but one area I'm interested in for my kids is personalized lesson planning. The science of learning and best practices around learning are generally well understood: spaced repetition, Socratic method, and idea synthesis. But most classrooms just aren't implementing the best version of the science because they need to standardize their teaching to the whole room.

As part of being the change I want to see, I put an hour into trying to help my first grade daughter do better on her spelling tests. She did so well in kindergarten on reading and writing, but they focused entirely on phonics and so the spelling tests in first grade came in very hot. For the fall semester I just hoped my daughter would come up to speed from the work she did in class. But I finally knew I had to do something when she spelled drawing as "jroling". So now I sit with her in the evenings a couple nights a week and do spelling practice. One on one time with any of my kids is a luxury right now, so I enjoy the opportunity to sit with her and watch her spelling skills improve.

To help streamline our studying and get me as much one on one time with my daughter as I can, I made this Claude skill to produce worksheets for us to go over. Here's the result below:

The skill takes a word list, groups the words phonetically, generates SVG illustrations for concrete nouns, and produces a week of print-ready worksheets — three practice days building in difficulty, plus a test page. Everything prints in black and white. To use it, go to claude.ai, open Customizations, and load the skill file. Then just give Claude a word list and it does the rest.
